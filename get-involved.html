The Group Chapters

The chapters are categorized into two streams:

a) The University / Innovation Chapter;

b) The Grassroot Chapter

University / Innovation Chapter

PCP Africa is currently working with existing and/or establishing novel innovation hubs across 5 strategic universities in Kenya including University of Nairobi, Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT), Strathmore University, Pwani University, and Maseno University.

The first three are located in Kenya’s capital, Nairobi
whereas the other two are located in the coastal and
western regions of Kenya respectively.

PCP Africa Group envisages growing its presence across at least 20 Universities in East Africa by 2025 and over 50 university or innovation chapters across Africa by 2030.

The University Innovation Hubs

The University Chapter, through the various University
Hubs, are responsible for creating open-access hardware
and software that will feed back into the development of student-learning programmes as well as offer innovative mentorship to grassroot learning labs.

The University Hubs have high degrees of autonomy and exercise self-determination (albeit in collaboration with
grassroot chapters) - both in designing and driving impact innovation &and sustainable programmes that feed into
the broader PCP Africa Group vision.

The PCP Africa Group acts as a strategic link between the University Chapters and the global / regional technology networks (GRTNs). The Group shall also support the
chapters’ programmes and offer strategic guidance and mentorship to the chapter focal leaders, students, and partner cohorts.

The programmes of the University Chapters are strategically aligned with the overarching vision of PCP
Africa Group - breaking walls, building horizontals, and democratizing access to computing in Africa. In particular, these chapters shall be driven by the need to enhance and sustain the preparedness of the African University students for the great digital economy and transition. In the long run, the programme will have as many students as possible accessing and leveraging the platform for limitless opportunities.

Grassroot Learning Chapter

Even as the international community’s focus shifts towards emerging technologies, the computer penetration (both hardware and software) into remote and local communities across Africa is still below par. This is despite the many attempts by governments and non-state actors (NSAs) to increase infrastructure capitation and in particular, construct and equip laboratories with computers. Unfortunately, a majority of such efforts tend to focus on urban computer literacy or if remote, are predominantly implemented in areas profiled as ‘urban-rural.’ The programmes of the age such as coding bootcamps, coding competitions, and artificiaL intelligence (AI) games among others, bear a huge risk of leaving behind and/or out, local communities in the remote rural areas despite carrying a huge talent base needed in achieving just digital transition.

PCP Africa Group leverages the US-based Kano Computing Platform and associated partners in enhancing computing access & opportunities for the talents at the local levels where digital impact is most required. We are particularly taking computing power to the underprivileged communities across Africa. In 2022, the grassroot chapter was launched in Kenyan counties of Samburu, Kisumu, Siaya, and Nairobi’s peripheral area of Kibra.

Grassroot Learning & Innovation Labs (G-LILs)

The Grassroot Chapter derives its innovation impetus from the University Chapter - both in terms of accelerating innovating mentorships for and/or fostering uptake of country-built verticals by junior and senior secondary schools. Also, we are collaborating with other stakeholders in setting up community learning labs at the grassroot levels for whose programs are closely supported by the University Innovation Hubs. The two chapters are involved in co-designing and implementing the programmes together with all the grassroot and university stakeholders in country-specific contexts. They include but are not limited to heads of schools, community clubs and libraries, university innovation labs, and student bazaars.

In essence, the University chapters work closely with the grassroot chapters in not just admitting the young cohorts into the programme but also innovatively offering them the needed mentorship. In the coming years, the programme seeks to progressively have as many young talents as possible in the G-LILs to help drive the impact agenda at local levels yet with global reach.
